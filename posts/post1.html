Why I Left Tech — And Why I’m Coming Back

Posted on April 20, 2025

For nearly a decade, I lived and breathed software. As a project manager at FPT Software, I led teams, met deadlines, and delivered solutions to clients across the globe. I loved solving problems. But eventually, something was missing. I craved a different kind of challenge — something more physical, more tangible.

So I made a bold decision: I left the tech industry and became a bus driver. Later, I entered a pre-apprenticeship program in the skilled trades through the Electrical Training Institute. It might sound like a strange leap, but it was one of the most eye-opening experiences of my life.

What I Learned on the Job Site

In construction, everything is about precision and safety. You don’t just "deploy and fix later" — a mistake can hurt someone. I learned to respect tools, follow processes, and work in teams where trust is everything. These are lessons I’ll carry with me into my next chapter in tech.

Why I’m Returning to Software

Even while I was in the field, I found myself tinkering with code at night. I missed creating. I missed solving abstract problems. And I realized: I didn’t need to choose between the worlds I loved. My goal now is to combine my technical knowledge, field experience, and leadership skills to build tools that matter — tools that serve real people.

I'm currently rebuilding my development portfolio, learning modern web technologies again, and excited to contribute to software teams that value curiosity, resilience, and real-world perspective.

Thanks for reading. If you're someone who's changed paths — or thinking about it — I’d love to hear your story, too.
