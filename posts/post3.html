From Bus Driver to Builder to Coder

Posted on March 25, 2025

When people see my resume, they often pause. “You went from managing software teams... to driving buses?” Yes — and I learned a lot from every turn.

Driving Taught Me Patience

Operating a bus in city traffic is no joke. You learn to stay calm, follow strict routes, and communicate with all kinds of people. It’s customer service, logistics, and discipline — all at once.

Building Taught Me Respect

Joining the Electrical Training Institute’s pre-apprenticeship program opened my eyes to the complexity of physical work. Measuring, bending, drilling — it all takes precision, planning, and teamwork. Construction taught me to slow down and pay attention to detail.

Software Still Feels Like Home

Despite all this, I kept feeling the pull of programming. I missed the creativity, the puzzles, the feeling of bringing something to life with code. That’s why I’ve returned to software, stronger than before and more motivated than ever.

There’s Power in a Nonlinear Path

Every job I’ve taken — bus driver, builder, developer — has shaped how I think and solve problems. And that diversity of experience? It’s not a weakness. It’s my biggest strength.

If you’ve taken a winding path too, embrace it. You might just be collecting the best tools for the next chapter.
